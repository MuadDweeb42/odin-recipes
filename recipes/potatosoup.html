Slow Cook Potato Soup

"Po-tay-toes. Boil 'em, mash 'em, stick 'em in a stew."

This hearty soup will give you the energy to conquer your own Mount Doom.

    Ingredients
  • 2 T unsalted butter
  • 1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 1/2 lb russet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 1/2 c vegetable broth
  • 1 1/2 c milk
  • 1/2 t celery seeds
  • 1/2 t dried thyme
  • 2 T corn starch
  • 1/2 c heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper, tot aste
  • Fresh minced chives
  • Shredded cheddar cheese
  • 4 slices of bacon, crispy, chopped
    Instructions
  1. In a large skillet over medium heat, melt the butter. Add the onion and cook until soft and translucent. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds, or until fragrant. Remove from heat.
  2. To the bowl of a slow cooker, add the onion mixture, potatoes, carrots, broth, milk, celery seeds, and thyme. Cover and cook on low for 6 hours or until potatoes are fork tender.
  3. Remove 2 tablespoons of the soup liquid and whisk in the cornstarch. Whisk back into soup and cook for another 10 minutes. Add cream, season to taste with salt and pepper, and heat for 5 more minutes.
  4. Serve topped with minced chives, cheddar, and bacon.
